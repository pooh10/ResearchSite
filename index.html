ReflectAI

AI-assisted journaling to support emotional awareness and reflective practice

Research by Pujan Pokharel • Advisor: Dr. Tim Gorichanaz • Drexel University

Jump to Results How we ran the study

Abstract

ReflectAI is a prototype journaling platform that integrates AI to encourage emotional awareness and reflective practice. In collaboration with Drexel University, we conducted a longitudinal study with six participants over 10–14 days. Participants journaled regularly with AI-generated prompts, completing pre- and post-surveys as well as optional interviews. Findings indicate that ReflectAI reduced blank-page anxiety, fostered deeper reflection, and provided supportive, non-judgmental guidance. Quantitative measures showed a slight increase in self-reported confidence in emotional understanding, while qualitative feedback highlighted usability and opportunities for improvement. This research offers early evidence that AI can meaningfully support personal reflection and emotional growth, laying the groundwork for future studies in digital journaling and human–AI interaction.

Keywords: AI, journaling, reflection, emotional awareness, human–AI interaction

Results

Across six participants, ReflectAI was used most frequently on phone/tablet devices, with an average of 4–5 sessions per participant over the study period. Average openness to reflection before the study was 3.67, and average self-reported confidence in understanding emotions after using the app was 3.83, indicating a slight positive change. Participants reported that ReflectAI helped them organize thoughts, provided clear prompts, and offered a supportive space for emotional exploration. Common improvement suggestions included enhanced visual design, easier access to past reflections, and more customizable prompts.

Discussion

Interpretation

These findings suggest that AI can play a meaningful role in supporting reflective practice. While modest in scale, the results align with prior work on expressive writing and digital journaling, providing a foundation for future exploration of human–AI interaction in personal growth.

Limitations

  • Small sample size (n=6)
  • Short study duration (10-14 days)
  • Self-reported measures

Future Work

  • Better visual design (decorative notebook feel)
  • Ability to access and read past reflections more easily
  • Customizable AI prompts
  • Offline mode for journaling without internet
  • Search function for past entries

Conclusion

ReflectAI demonstrates the potential of AI-assisted journaling to reduce blank-page anxiety, promote deeper reflection, and provide supportive guidance for emotional awareness. Although the study was small in scale, participants consistently reported that the app encouraged clarity and offered value in reflective practice. These findings highlight both the promise of AI in personal growth contexts and the importance of continued research into usability, customization, and long-term impact. Future work will refine the design, expand participant diversity, and explore how AI can complement—not replace—human support in reflective and therapeutic practices.
